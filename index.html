About Me

My name is Adeniyi Dada Israel, a proud Nigerian from Ilesha West Local Government Area, Osun State. I've spent most of my life in Ilorin, Kwara State, where my journey in education started.

I began my teacher training at Kinsey College of Education, where I earned a Nigerian Certificate in Education (NCE) in Christian Religious Studies and English. Currently, I'm a final year student (400 level) at Kwara State University, Malete, pursuing a B.Ed. in Early Childhood and Primary Education.

Personal Statement

As an emerging educator, I am deeply committed to fostering holistic development in young learners through innovative, child-centered approaches. My teaching practice is rooted in the belief that every child deserves access to quality education that nurtures their cognitive, social-emotional, and physical growth.

Through my academic preparation and practical experiences, I have developed a robust skill set in early childhood pedagogy, inclusive education strategies, and community engagement. I am particularly passionate about creating learning environments that celebrate diversity and promote active, play-based learning.

My professional journey reflects my dedication to continuous improvement and reflective practice. I actively seek opportunities to enhance my teaching methods through professional development and collaboration with colleagues, parents, and community stakeholders.

Looking ahead, I aspire to contribute to educational innovation in Nigeria by implementing evidence-based practices that address the unique needs of young learners while respecting cultural contexts and local resources.

Reflective Practice

As a developing educator, I maintain a reflective journal to document my teaching experiences, analyze challenges, and plan professional growth. This ongoing practice helps me improve my instructional strategies and classroom management skills.

What Worked Well

  • Multisensory Phonics Instruction: Successfully implemented tactile letter cards that improved recognition for 80% of struggling readers
  • Classroom Management: Established clear routines that reduced transition time by 50%
  • Parent Engagement: Developed weekly learning updates that increased parent participation in home activities
  • Differentiated Instruction: Created tiered math activities that accommodated varying skill levels

Areas Needing Improvement

  • Time Management: Often overplanned lessons, leaving insufficient time for closure activities
  • Assessment Techniques: Need more varied formative assessment strategies beyond verbal questioning
  • Technology Integration: Limited use of digital tools to enhance learning experiences
  • Special Needs Adaptation: Required more individualized modifications for students with learning differences

My Improvement Plan

  • For Time Management: I will pilot timed lesson segments with visual reminders for transitions
  • For Assessments: Planning to attend workshop on alternative assessment strategies
  • For Technology: Committing to learn one new educational app each month
  • For Special Needs: I will engage in learning from special education teacher for some hours
